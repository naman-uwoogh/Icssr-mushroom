About the Project

Bringing Literature,
Technology and Livelihood

This ICSSR-funded study investigates the potential of affordable IoT based monitoring system to enable sustainable mushroom cultivation as a livelihood option for rural women who currently have no awareness or access of it.

The Problem

Mushroom cultivation offers significant livelihood potential, yet the majority of rural women in our study area had never encountered it. Commercial IoT monitoring tools that improve yield reliability cost ₹5,000–20,000, which is entirely inaccessible to smallholder farmers.

Our Approach

We first went to the field to understand the reality faced by women farmers before designing any technology. This approach ensures our solution addresses real barriers rather than assumed ones.

The Solution

A plug-and-play IoT monitoring kit costing around ₹1,500 that requires no technical knowledge to set up. Any woman with a basic smartphone can connect it via a simple captive portal — no IT support, no technical training needed.

Project Objectives

  1. Assess baseline awareness and feasibility of mushroom cultivation among rural women in Delhi, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan
  2. Design and validate an affordable IoT monitoring kit appropriate for non-technical rural users
  3. Identify socioeconomic barriers to technology adoption and propose contextually appropriate solutions
  4. Establish a scalable deployment model suitable for Self Help Groups and cooperative settings
  5. Contribute to the evidence base for technology-enabled livelihood interventions targeting rural women in India
Why This Matters

The Challenges
We Set Out to Solve

Our field interactions across four states revealed a consistent picture of unmet potential — women eager for income opportunities but facing compounding barriers.

Zero Awareness

The majority of women we met had no knowledge that mushrooms could be cultivated commercially at home. The knowledge gap predates any technology barrier.

Cost Exclusion

Commercial mushroom monitoring systems cost ₹15,000–50,000. For a household earning ₹8,000–12,000 per month, this is completely inaccessible without subsidy.

Digital Literacy Gap

Most existing IoT agricultural tools require significant technical setup. Women with basic smartphone access cannot deploy them without external technical support.

Connectivity Limits

Rural areas across our study states have variable internet access. A solution that fails when the WiFi router changes is not a reliable livelihood tool.

Research Gap

A systematic review of literature found no published study addressing IoT mushroom cultivation in the context of women's empowerment or rural Indian deployment.

Policy Disconnect

NABARD and state government schemes promote mushroom cultivation for SHGs but lack low-cost monitoring tools to support consistent, reliable yields at scale.

The Technology

A Kit Built Around
Real Constraints

Every design decision was made in direct response to what we heard in the field. The result is a monitoring kit that works for the people who are in need of it.

IoT Monitoring Kit

Temperature · Humidity · CO₂

  • Plug and play WiFi setup No technical knowledge needed. Phone connects to kit hotspot, user picks their WiFi network. Done.
  • Real-time sensor monitoring Temperature, humidity and CO₂ monitored continuously. Alerts sent to Android app when conditions go outside safe range.
  • Automated relay control Humidifier activates automatically when humidity drops. No manual intervention required during grow cycles.
  • Data recording and export All sensor readings stored and exportable as CSV for farmer records, loan applications or cooperative reporting.

Kit cost per unit

~₹1,500

Commercial equivalent: ₹15,000–50,000

Up to 97% more affordable
